Odysseus: “How didst thou dare to come down to Hades…?”

Aeneas: “Begin thy journey! Now, all thy courage!”

Dido: “Not by choice I fled thy shores…”

Tiresias: “Give place from the pit and draw back thy sharp sword…”

Anticlea: “It was longing for thee… that robbed me of honey-sweet life.”

Achilles: “I would rather serve another man as a field-hand… than be lord of all the dead.”

Anchises: “I cherished long this hope… What perils manifold assailed thee…”
